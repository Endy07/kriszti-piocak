What Is Hirudotherapy?

An Ancient Practice,
Modern Understanding

Where nature's pharmacy meets human wellness

Leech therapy — or hirudotherapy — is one of the oldest documented healing practices in human history. The leech is not merely a tool — it is a living bridge between nature's ancient wisdom and modern healing.

The leech's saliva contains over 80 bioactive compounds including hirudin, hyaluronidase, and histamine-like substances that work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, anticoagulant, and microcirculation-improving effects.

Beyond the biochemical effects, leech therapy has a profound energetic influence, helping to restore the body's natural equilibrium while providing deep relaxation and stress relief.

Important Contraindication

Leech therapy is strictly contraindicated if the patient is taking anticoagulants (blood thinners). Do not undergo treatment without full disclosure of all medications to the therapist. This also applies to beta-blockers and any blood pressure medication.

Preparation & Aftercare

Before & After Treatment

Everything you need to know

Before Treatment

What to Know Before Your Session

  • Arrive in dark-colored, loose clothing — the dressing may seep
  • Avoid scented shower gels, body lotions, and creams — leeches are sensitive to chemical smells
  • Do not smoke — leeches cannot tolerate tobacco smoke and will not attach
  • Disclose all medications — especially blood pressure medication, beta-blockers, and anticoagulants
  • Expect the session to last approximately 2 hours
After Treatment

Recovery & Aftercare

  • The therapist will dress the wound — remove after 24 hours
  • The wound may seep or bleed for up to 36 hours — keep sterile gauze at home
  • Drink plenty of fluids to help your body process the bioactive substances
  • No intensive activity on the day of treatment — driving and long walks not recommended
  • For itching, redness, or swelling: icing, Fenistil gel, or calcium effervescent tablets
  • Rarely, fever or chills may occur — contact your therapist if concerned
