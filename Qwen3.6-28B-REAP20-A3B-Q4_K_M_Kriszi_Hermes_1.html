Important Contraindication

Leech therapy is strictly contraindicated if the patient is taking anticoagulants (blood thinners). Do not undergo treatment without full disclosure of all medications to the therapist. This applies to beta-blockers and any blood pressure medication as well.

Preparation & Aftercare

Before & After Treatment

Everything you need to know before your session and during the recovery period.

✏ Before Treatment

What should I wear to the appointment?

+

Arrive in comfortable, dark-colored, loose clothing. The dressing is thick and may seep — dark colors are essential to avoid embarrassment.

Can I use scented products before treatment?

+

No. Avoid scented shower gels, body lotions, and creams before your session. Leeches are sensitive to chemical smells and may refuse to attach.

Can I smoke before treatment?

+

Do not smoke! Leeches cannot tolerate tobacco smoke and will not attach if you have smoked. Please refrain from smoking before and during your session.

What medications must I disclose?

+

You must inform the therapist if you take blood pressure medication, beta-blockers, or anticoagulants. This is critical for your safety.

☐ During Treatment

How long does a session take?

+

One session takes approximately 2 hours. The therapist will carefully place the leeches and monitor the process.

Is it painful?

+

The bite feels like a mild, stinging pain — most patients find it very tolerable. The leeches are initially applied sparingly, and their number is later increased tailored to the individual, depending on your reaction.

How many sessions do I need?

+

Generally, 10 treatments on a weekly basis are recommended. Individual variations may occur depending on your condition and response.

✍ After Treatment

When can I remove the dressing?

+

The therapist will dress the wound with a thick, absorbent layer. This should only be removed after 24 hours have passed.

How long will the wound bleed?

+

The wound may seep or bleed for up to 36 hours. Keep sterile gauze and adhesive bandages at home to change the dressing if it bleeds through. This is a natural and expected part of the process.

In exceptional cases, if bleeding persists beyond 24 hours, Hyperol tablets may be recommended for hemostasis. Consult your therapist.

Can I exercise after treatment?

+

Any intensive or strenuous activity is prohibited on the day of treatment. Driving or long walks are not recommended as sluggishness or drowsiness may occur.

What should I drink after treatment?

+

Drink plenty of fluids! Hydration helps your body process the leech's bioactive substances and supports recovery.

What about itching and swelling?

+

Scratching or picking at the wound is strictly forbidden. For itching, redness, tension, or swelling:

  • Apply icing or cold water compresses
  • Fenistil gel can be used
  • For severe symptoms: calcium effervescent tablets or a Calcimusc injection ampoule

Can I get a fever after treatment?

+

Rarely, fever or chills may occur — this usually indicates an underlying illness that was already latent in the body. These aftereffects may appear on the day of treatment or even a week later. Contact your therapist if concerned.
